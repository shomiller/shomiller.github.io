About Me:

I am a second year PhD student in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Stony Brook. I did my undergraduate work at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, where I graduated in 2015 with a B.S. in Physics and a B.S. in Mathematics.

I'm interested in quantum field theory and it's application to physics beyond the standard model (BSM). In particular, I focus on phenomenological and theoretical aspects of BSM physics, including electroweak physics, dark matter, supersymmetry, baryogenesis, and their signatures at the LHC and other colliders.

Some more information about me is available in my CV.
